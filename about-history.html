Our History

From inauguration to full Ward E coverage — the story of how the MFA Ambassadors came to be.

How It All Began

Our Story

A Movement Born from Community

The MFA Ambassadors were formally established on June 13, 2025 — born from a shared belief that Rt. Hon. Mojeed Fatai Adebola's continued representation in the Lagos State House of Assembly is the surest path to sustained development for every community in Ward E, Ibeju-Lekki Constituency I.

🏛️
June 13, 2025

Foundation & Inauguration

Formal inauguration held at the flag-off ceremony in the presence of Rt. Hon. MFA, APC leaders, and the Ward E community. The Central Executive Committee (CEC) pledge was taken, formally establishing the Ambassadors as the official grassroots movement for the campaign.

🚶
Mobilisation

Town-to-Town Campaign

The Ambassador network was deployed across all 12 communities of Ward E. Each community received direct engagement: door-to-door visits, voter conversations, feedback collection, and political awareness sessions. No community was left behind.

📢
Key Milestones

Poster Presentation & Courtesy Visit

4,000 campaign posters were formally presented to the Executive Chairman at the Council Secretariat. A subsequent courtesy visit announced the flag-bearer unveiling and demonstrated the growing structure and momentum of the campaign.

📱
Ongoing

Weekly Media & Outreach

The Ambassadors maintain a weekly WhatsApp broadcast programme reaching thousands across the constituency. Social media — Instagram, Facebook, and X (Twitter) — is actively managed by the Media & Communication directorate. The structure is stronger than ever.

Our Slogan

"One With God Is A Majority"

Ileri Oluwa Dan Dan Ni. This is the slogan that unites us. It is not merely a phrase — it is the conviction that drives every door knocked, every poster distributed, and every community meeting held across Ward E. We campaign not from ambition alone, but from faith and purpose.
