Rt. Hon. Mojeed Fatai Adebola

Chief Whip, Lagos State House of Assembly

Who He Is

A Legislator Rooted in Service

Rt. Hon. Mojeed Fatai Adebola — popularly known as MFA — is a four-term Member of the Lagos State House of Assembly representing Ibeju-Lekki Constituency I under the All Progressives Congress (APC). He currently serves as the Chief Whip of the House, a position that places him at the heart of legislative coordination in Lagos State.

Across four terms in office, MFA has championed constituency development in roads, education, welfare, and grassroots political representation. His work has directly touched the lives of residents across all 12 communities in Ward E, Ibeju-Lekki Constituency I — from Abijo and Awoyaya to Eputu, Lakowe, and beyond.

He is not only a legislator — he is a community builder. His consistent presence in Ward E, his accessibility to constituents, and his track record of delivering on his promises have made him the most trusted political figure in the constituency.

The MFA Ambassadors exist because of what he stands for: a fifth term that continues this legacy of service, continuity, and community-first representation in the Lagos State House of Assembly.

His Vision for a Fifth Term

MFA's campaign for a fifth term is built on continuity and growth. The work is not finished. Roads still need to be built. Schools still need support. Communities still need a strong, experienced voice in the Lagos State House of Assembly. The MFA Ambassadors exist to carry this message — community by community, door by door, household by household — across every corner of Ward E.

"One With God Is A Majority. We are not just campaigning — we are carrying a community mandate back to the House of Assembly."
— Rt. Hon. Mojeed Fatai Adebola (MFA)
