MFA Ambassadors' Manifesto

Anchored in the conviction that Rt. Hon. MFA's return benefits every resident of Ibeju-Lekki Constituency I. Our work drives four core pillars.

Our Mission

Purpose & Values

What We Stand For

The MFA Ambassadors exist to carry a community mandate — not just a political campaign. Every activity we run, every community we visit, and every message we broadcast is guided by four non-negotiable pillars.

🌱

1. Grassroots Mobilisation

We go where the people are — town by town, community by community — ensuring every voter across Ibeju-Lekki Constituency I knows MFA's name, record, and vision for a fifth term. No community is too small or too far.

📚

2. Political Awareness

MFA's four-term record in roads, education, welfare, and representation is communicated as real stories — concrete outcomes that residents have seen and felt. We inform, not just campaign.

🤝

3. Strategic Alignment

The Ambassadors operate in coordination with APC structures at the ward, local government, and state levels. Disciplined, unified, and accountable — we move as one.

📱

4. Digital Communication

Weekly WhatsApp broadcasts and active social media presence — Instagram, Facebook, X (Twitter) — ensure MFA's message reaches every screen and every conversation across the constituency.

The Blueprint

Our Model

A Replicable Model for Community-Driven Politics

The MFA Ambassadors are more than a campaign team. We are a blueprint — a replicable model for community-driven political communication across the Epe Division and beyond. Built on June 13, 2025, and growing stronger every week.

The Blueprint in Practice

Clear leadership structure with defined roles. Community coverage that reaches every household. Digital outreach that keeps the movement alive between physical engagements. APC alignment that ensures every action serves the broader mission. These are not aspirations — they are the daily practice of the MFA Ambassadors.

"One With God Is A Majority. We are not just campaigning — we are carrying a community mandate back to the House of Assembly."
— Rt. Hon. Mojeed Fatai Adebola (MFA)
